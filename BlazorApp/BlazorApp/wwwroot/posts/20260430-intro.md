I'm a software developer with a background in structural engineering. My career started
in concrete and steel — connections, load paths, fabrication coordination — but over time
the software side took over. These days I spend most of my time writing code, and the
engineering background mostly shows up in what I choose to build and how I think about
the problems.

This site is a hobby project. Some of it will be interactive tools, some of it will be
writing about things I've been working through. It's not a portfolio and it's not a blog
in any disciplined sense — more of a running record of what I've been making and thinking
about.

A lot of the content here is written with AI assistance. I've never been a strong writer,
and if AI is what it takes to get ideas out of my head and onto a page, I'm fine with
that. This site is personal — I'm not worried about the optics. The ideas are mine; the
prose just has a collaborator.

More broadly, this is a space to explore how AI can augment domain expertise. That
intersection — where a specialist's knowledge meets what these tools can do — is what
I find most interesting right now.

If something here is useful to you, that's a bonus.
